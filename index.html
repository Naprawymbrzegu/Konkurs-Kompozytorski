Regulations

1. Organizers
The competition is organized by: Andrzej Krzanowski Foundation, Small Music Academy in Czechowice-Dziedzice, Municipal Culture Center in Czechowice-Dziedzice.

2. Competition Purpose
The purpose of the competition is to promote the work of young composers and enrich the contemporary accordion repertoire.

3. Participants
The competition is open to composers up to 35 years of age (born after October 1, 1990), regardless of nationality.

4. Participation Rules
Each participant may submit one original composition.
The work:
- must be previously unpublished, not performed publicly, and not awarded,
- should last from 6 to 8 minutes,
- must be written for solo accordion, with optional use of electronics (e.g., stereo tape, live electronics).
The organizer does not provide realization of the electronic layer during the concert ‚Äì it should be self-sufficient or possible to perform by the accordionist.

5. Submission Deadline and Method
Submission deadline: October 1, 2025 (the date of message receipt is decisive).
Results announcement: October 28, 2025 during the "Triptych" concert in Czechowice-Dziedzice.
Applications should be sent exclusively electronically to: competition@krzanowski.org
The application should include:
- score in PDF format (marked with a motto ‚Äì pseudonym),
- audio recording or rendering (if available),
- text file (form) with author's data (name and surname, date of birth, email address, phone number, short biography), also marked with the motto,
- declaration:
"I declare that I am the author of the submitted work, which has not been performed, published, or awarded before. I accept the competition regulations."
Participation in the competition requires payment of an entry fee of 200 PLN to the account number: 12 3456 7890 1234 5678 9012 3456

6. Jury
The jury composition is listed above. It includes recognized artists representing the contemporary music community. Jury decisions are final and indisputable.

7. Prizes
The total prize pool is 10,000 PLN.
The awarded compositions will be premiered during the Autumn Musical Festival Alkagran in 2026.
The jury reserves the right to distribute prizes differently or not award them.

8. Copyright
Participants retain full copyright to their submitted compositions.
Organizers obtain the right to:
- perform, record, and document the awarded works,
- make their recordings available free of charge for promotional and archival purposes.

9. Final Provisions
Submitting a work means full acceptance of these regulations.
Organizers reserve the right to introduce changes to the regulations (not affecting the principle of equal treatment of participants) or to cancel the competition for important reasons.
In matters not regulated by these regulations, the organizers decide.

10. Personal Data Protection (GDPR)
The administrator of participants' personal data is the Andrzej Krzanowski Foundation based in Czechowice-Dziedzice.
Data will be processed for the purpose of conducting the competition, contacting participants, announcing results, and promoting the event.
Providing data is voluntary but necessary to participate in the competition.
Participants have the right to access data, correct it, delete it, limit processing, transfer it, withdraw consent, and file a complaint with the President of UODO.
Detailed information can be obtained by contacting the administrator at: info@krzanowski.org
